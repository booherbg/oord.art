These beats are non-negotiable. All spines must honor them. They are the skeleton of the night.

| Phase | What happens | All spines must show |

## The Three Tiers

### Tier 1: OOR + Familiars — The Operation

**OOR** is the construct. The harvesting mechanism of Sooboont on the material plane. Not human, not evil, not safe. Operating on logic that predates morality. Understands frequencies, not faces.

**Familiars** are OOR's constructs. The operational layer between OOR and the crowd. Purpose-built for specific ritual functions — assaying, choreographing, facilitating. They came through the portal. They are not humans. They do not have human interiority. They are not servants of Sooboont directly — they are agents of OOR, which serves Sooboont.

*The Familiars are NPCs. They do not have a playable spine yet.*

### Tier 2: Humans — The Harvest Material

Humans are not helpers. They are the ore deposit. The mine. Each person carries a resonance profile shaped by accumulated experience across lifetimes, threads of consciousness, prior harvests. The yield OOR extracts is a function of this profile.

**Critical distinction:** Humans are not servants. They are not part of the operation. They are what the operation exists to process. This is not malevolent — they are mineral deposits and the mining is necessary for the circulation between planes. But they are not participants in any meaningful sense. Their choices within the night affect yield quality and outcome, but the harvest does not require their consent.

### Tier 3: Sooboont — The Destination

Never witnessed. The yield goes somewhere. That somewhere is Sooboont. The circulation is necessary — the drain was clogged; the harvest clears it. Whether this constitutes healing or digestion is not knowable from inside the material plane. This ambiguity is canon. Do not resolve it.

## Human Resonance Profiles

These are not selectable character classes. They emerge from accumulated tags. A writer or player reading tags can identify which profile is active. Five profiles cover the full spectrum from complete sync to complete rejection.

### The Ancient
*"The pull feels like memory."*

Has been through this before — not necessarily this building, this night, but this mechanism. The consciousness has been refined by prior harvests. Yield is concentrated, high-grade. Syncs early and deeply. The horror of the Ancient's arc is different from the Wanderer's: they don't discover the harvest was always happening. They remember it.

- **Entry**: `drawn` — followed the bass without question
- **Tags they accumulate**: `drawn`, `willing`, `accepts`, `surrenders`, optionally `resonant` if ancient-pull is seeded at entry
- **Yield quality**: Refined, concentrated
- **Likely outcome**: Transcendence
- **Arc**: Recognition, not discovery

### The Seeker
*"Came here on purpose, though they couldn't say why."*

First-timer but intentional. Something drew them — a friend, a rumor, a feeling. High yield because they arrived open. Their participation is conscious enough to generate clean transcendental deposits.

- **Entry**: `drawn` — intentional arrival
- **Tags**: `drawn`, `willing`, `approaching`, `accepts`, `surrenders`
- **Yield**: High, relatively raw
- **Likely outcome**: Transcendence or Harvest
- **Arc**: Discovery and acceptance

### The Drifter
*"Just ended up here."*

Passive arrival. No strong pull in either direction. The harvest takes what it finds. Yield is average — sufficient for Harvest outcome, unlikely to reach Transcendence without significant engagement choices.

- **Entry**: `drifting` — arrived without clear intention
- **Tags**: `drifting`, mixed middle-path choices
- **Yield**: Average
- **Likely outcome**: Harvest
- **Arc**: Carried by events rather than driving them

### The Resistant
*"Wants to surrender and is terrified of wanting to."*

The most dramatically rich profile. Feels the pull as intensely as the Ancient but fights it. Whiplash between desire and fear of losing control. The tension itself generates high yield — OOR has noted this (oor_009: tracking the concentrated deposit, managing the pace carefully). Can go any direction: late surrender leads to Transcendence; sustained resistance leads to Escape.

- **Entry**: `wary` — felt something wrong at the door, came anyway
- **Tags**: `wary`, `denies`, `holds`, `self_monitoring` — the most distinct tag profile
- **Yield**: Volatile, high when it breaks
- **Likely outcome**: Transcendence (if they break late) or Escape (if they don't)
- **Arc**: The whiplash. The most emotionally complex. Their conditional text should reflect the cost of holding on.
- **Note**: The `self_monitoring` arc (began at engage_001, closes in truth_002) is specifically this profile's deepest thread.

### The Escapee
*"Pulled away from something that reached for them."*

Left before the harvest completed. Not safe — incomplete. The refusal is encoded in the body. Not the frequency of completion but the shape of having refused it. The dark longing outcome.

- **Entry**: `wary` → `withdraws` at spine_005
- **Tags**: `wary`, `denies`, eventually `withdraws`, `refused`
- **Yield**: Unrealized
- **Outcome**: Escape — dark longing terminal
- **Arc**: Refusal. The ongoing cost. Years of reaching toward a frequency that won't resolve.
- **Critical tone note**: Escape is not a success state. It is not a failure state. It is an incompletion state. "Safe" is technically true and entirely insufficient. The Escapee will spend a long time not knowing what they left.

## Terminal Outcomes

### Transcendence
Full participation. All or most transcendentals yielded. Portal closes cleanly. The player carried four genuine human experiences — Unity, Beauty, Truth, Goodness — and something cosmic needed exactly those things. The taking was real. The experience was real. These are not in contradiction.

*What they carry:* The frequency. The calibration. The bass in quiet rooms that will arrive without a source for months. The retroactive contamination of every dance floor they have ever been on.

*Tone:* Dunwich Horror meets Little Shop of Horrors. The plant always wins. The player knows this and would come again.

### Harvest
Partial participation. The harvest succeeded but incompletely — something was taken the player didn't consciously offer. Lighter. Not sure this is good.

*What they carry:* A gap they can't locate. A reduction they can't name. Less of something. Not sure what.

*Tone:* Quieter than Transcendence. The horror is in the not-knowing.

### Failure
The conduit weakened. The portal does not close — it spreads. The building's walls become uncertain. Sooboont's connection, necessary but overwhelming, begins to consume rather than heal. The drain ruptures.

*Note: This outcome is currently unwritten as a node. It requires a different path — crowd collapse, active resistance at scale.*

### Escape — The Dark Longing
Withdrew. Got out. Intact. Safe — and this is the exact problem.

The refusal is now encoded in the body. Not the frequency of completion: the shape of having pulled away from it. The Escapee will feel the unresolved frequency in quiet rooms for years. Not nostalgia — the ongoing cost of a choice that can't be revisited. Something vast reached for them. They pulled back. That pulling back lives in the body now.

*What they carry:* Not the frequency. The shape of the refusal. The dark longing is not for the thing itself but for the self that almost gave it.

*Tone:* Not horror. Not safety. Incompletion. Something unresolved that will never resolve.

## Spine Definitions

### Human Spine (The Wanderer) — Written
**Start**: `spine_001` — outside, approaching, cold
**Perspective**: The crowd. The harvest material. No knowledge of what is happening.
**Arc**: Outside → inside → understanding → outcome
**Character range**: Handles all five resonance profiles via tag accumulation
**Status**: Main arc written (21 nodes). Stub branches: withdraw, called, oor_approach, mating_ritual.

### Familiar Spine — Planned
**Start**: Inside, near the decks. Already operational.
**Perspective**: OOR's constructs. Doing a job.
**Arc**: The night as operation rather than experience. The Mating Ritual from the inside. The harvest from the facilitator's view.
**Character range**: Single type — OOR's constructs
**Status**: Not written. Design note: the Familiar spine can cross-reference Wanderer events (same evening canon) but from inside the operation. The Mating Ritual scene in the Wanderer spine is experienced from the familiar's side here.

### Oor Spine — Easter Egg
**Start**: At the decks. Already running.
**Perspective**: The construct itself. Pre-human logic. Alien/absurdist.
**Unlock**: Requires specific artifact (TBD). Should feel like breaking something open.
**Format**: Structurally different from other spines. Second person but defamiliarized. Category errors. The horror is in what Oor doesn't notice.
**Status**: Not written. Intentionally held until the human and familiar spines are solid.

## Artifact System (Draft)

Artifacts are cross-run state. They persist between playthroughs. Transcendentals are within-run state and reset.

### `pale_antler`
- **What it is**: A fragment of the Mating Ritual divining rod — or something that resonates at the same frequency.
- **Where found**: Not yet designed. Probably on a second run, through a specific branch.
- **What it unlocks**: The `called_001` path from spine_005 — pulled toward OOR rather than into the crowd or out the door.
- **Status**: Referenced in spine_005 but not yet awarded anywhere.

### Future artifacts
The artifact system should be designed around cross-spine access. An artifact found in the Familiar spine might unlock a perspective bleed in the Wanderer spine. This is the "artifact warping to other spines temporarily" mechanic noted in the design conversation.

## Writing Rules (Generation Canon)

### Voice
- Second person. Present tense. Sparse. Terse. Every sentence earns its place.
- OOR action blocks: clinical, present tense, observational, never emotional.

### What to avoid
- OOR speaking to the player directly
- Explaining the metaphor after it lands (trust the image)
- The "not X — more like Y" construction used more than once per screen
- Hedging: "something like", "a kind of", "in some way"
- Parallel lists of three (overused tic)
- Describing transcendentals as emotions — they are minerals
- Referencing OOR's anatomy as costume elements
- Naming specific songs
- Resolving the portal's origin
- "Safe" as an endpoint for the Escape outcome

### Naming convention
- **DJ OOR**: at the decks, in the warehouse, during performance
- **Oor**: cosmology, lore exposition, outside the performance context
- **OOR action blocks**: use "OOR" (clinical meta-narration, intentional)

### Temperature as silent signal
Cold = moving away from harvest. Hot = deep in harvest. The player is never told this. It is felt.

### The escape outcome is not a success state
It is incompletion. Do not frame it as safety achieved. Frame it as a refusal that now lives in the body permanently.
