## 1) Setting

### The Venue

A former industrial manufacturing facility in an unnamed rust belt city. Familiar enough to recognize, wrong enough to notice. The building has the bones of a factory (concrete floor, high ceilings, old iron fixtures that hum at frequencies no one can explain). It was built to make things; tonight it is making something else.

### The City

Unnamed, post-industrial, Midwestern. It could be Fargo; it is not Fargo. A city where the grid feels slightly off at the edges, where alleys lead somewhere unexpected, and where the tree line at the end of a block feels older than the buildings.

### Atmosphere

The familiar becomes twisted as the night deepens. Machinery in the walls responds to the music. By the end of the night the building is not where you thought it was.

### The Approach

Damp, cold, industrial edges. Alleys, gravel lots, orange sodium lights. Bass reaches the body before the building is visible. You feel it in the sternum, follow it, and the building finds you before you find it.

## 2) Entities (Three Tiers)

### SOOBOONT

#### Nature

Primary deity. Primordial. Pre-temporal. Older than age as a concept. Not malevolent; indifferent in the way a star is indifferent.

#### Function

A cosmic heart circulating transcendentals between the Primordial Plane and the material world. The connection is necessary (circulatory). Bass is the oldest frequency. The subwoofer is not a metaphor.

#### Awareness

Sooboont is not aware of individuals. Humans are mineral deposits containing needed material. This is not personal.

#### Canon quote

"Known primarily through its effects on those who encounter it. Never witnessed directly. Only felt."

#### Reference cluster

Azathoth, Ain Soph, Yog-Sothoth, The Demiurge, The Pleroma, Sophia, King in Yellow.

### OOR / DJ OOR

#### Role

Construct. Harvester. Operating mechanism of Sooboont on the material plane. At the decks: DJ OOR.

#### Origin

Not born; constructed. Primordial forces were forced into a functional material form that defies logic. Designed to function, not to be understood.

#### Nature

Ambivalent but terrifying. Not evil. Not safe. Operates on pre-moral logic. Understands frequencies, not people. Transcendentals are minerals to OOR. OOR is mining.

#### Appearance

Orange/yellow, furry and tinsel-bright. Mouth at center with tongue branching into many jointed limbs. Giant ear at top covered in eyes.

#### Canon quote

"From across the room it reads as comedy... up close the tongue-limbs flex at knuckle joints and the motion is peristaltic... one eye looks at you, the others track things behind you that you cannot see."

#### Voice rule

DJ OOR does not speak to player or crowd directly. No direct address. Music is the communication channel.

#### Arc rule

As OOR becomes more understandable, OOR itself becomes less frightening while the cosmology becomes more frightening. Understanding reveals retroactive contamination: the harvest has always been happening.

#### Naming convention

- Use **DJ OOR** at decks / in-warehouse / performance contexts.
- Use **Oor** in cosmology/lore exposition or outside performance context.

#### Reference cluster

The Faun, Dunwich Horror, Annihilation, The Hermit, Meow Wolf.

### The Antlered Companions (Familiars)

#### Role

OOR's constructs. Operational layer for assaying, choreographing, and facilitating ritual. Functional, not threatening, not human.

#### Origin

Came through the portal with OOR. Constructed for function. Agents of OOR (and through OOR, Sooboont). No human interiority. No human-style arc.

#### Human distinction (critical)

Humans are harvest material; familiars are harvest operation. Familiars do not yield transcendentals; they identify/facilitate yield in others.

#### Mating Ritual pair

One familiar carries Sooboont's divining rod (resonance assay); another choreographs. Together they identify those closest to yielding. This is assaying, not seduction.

#### Canon quote

"The crowd is a spectrum... first-timers to prior-harvest echoes... none are servants, none are operation staff, all are what the operation exists to process."

## 3) Transcendentals (4 Minerals)

Not emotions; harvestable properties of collective human experience. Single-person instances are insufficient. Portal requires collective density.

### Unity

- Essence: many bodies moving as one
- Floor expression: synchronized movement, call/response, dissolving individual boundary

### Beauty

- Essence: form transcending function
- Floor expression: perfect release/drop timed to embodied need

### Truth

- Essence: unselfconscious presence
- Floor expression: no performance membrane, no audience orientation

### Goodness

- Essence: care extended outward
- Floor expression: making space, offering hand, noticing/acting without reward

## 4) The Portal

### Visual language

Concentric distorted geometry (speaker membrane + organic anatomy), like Sooboont heartbeat rendered into space.

### Why here

Unknown. Explicitly unresolved. **Do not resolve this ambiguity.**

### Purpose

Healing/circulation restoration between planes. Flow had clogged; portal restores/increases circulation. Harvest as drainage, not theft.

### Mechanics

Crowd becomes conduit/circuit. Collective transcendentals are conductive material. DJ OOR is operator/equipment logic.

### Success state

Sufficient density, flow restored, portal closes cleanly, OOR returns.

### Failure state

Insufficient participation weakens conduit. Portal does not close; expands outward searching for density. Connection becomes destructive in excess (rupture, not clearing).

## 5) Rituals (5 Events)

### 01 - The Opening

Portal opens, bass begins, room changes. Player may arrive after opening and only feel aftershock.

Tone: silly but unsettling.

### 02 - The Mating Ritual

Familiar with divining rod + choreographer familiar move through crowd, resonating latent transcendent force and identifying near-yield participants.

Tone: pagan harvest energy; operation interpreted by crowd as play.

### 03 - The DJ OOR Slide

Collective movement ritual via robotic/vocoded call-response. Crowd follows reflexively; unity spikes harvest.

Tone: absurd and effective; comedy is part of mechanism.

### 04 - The Dance Circle

Light circle + spinning arrow selecting participants. Selection is not random; it "finds" who must be exposed.

Tone: low-stakes party form with high-stakes exposure mechanics.

### 05 - The Closing

Harvest completion via collective descent to floor; bass reduces to low drone; then stillness; drone drops; silence; portal closes; DJ OOR gone.

Tone: body-memory ending, not plot-memory ending.

## 6) Primordial Plane

### Description

Realm prior/separate from material reality. No ordinary sound/color/form categories. Transcendentals originate and are pulled back here. Cycle vs digestion remains unresolved from inside perspective.

### Player experience

Perspective branches/artifact unlocks may expose player to cognitive/systemic instability:

- orientation failure
- second-person voice malfunction
- text corruption
- choice disappearance or unreadable-language choices
- stillness with directional pressure

This is exposure, not transcendence.

### Sooboont presence

Not visible as figure; present as medium (pressure/atmosphere/rhythmic compression).

## 8) Terminal Outcomes

### Transcendence

Full participation, clean closure. Body retains foreign frequency; later quiet-room bass afterimages persist.

### Harvest

Partial participation. Harvest succeeds but includes non-conscious offering. Felt as lighter/ambiguous loss.

### Failure

Conduit weakens, portal remains open and spreads, environment destabilizes, necessary connection becomes consuming.

### Escape

Withdrawal is intactness plus long-tail burden. Refusal becomes embodied pattern ("shape of refusal"), not simple safety.
