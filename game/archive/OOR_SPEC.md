## PART ONE: LORE & CANON

### The Event Context

DJ OOR is the performance art persona of a two-person DJ act performing at the **Plains Art Museum Spring Gala 2026 — "Nightmare at the Museum"**, Fargo, North Dakota. The act was not solicited; it is a self-directed art project. The performance combines projection mapping, synchronized LED lighting, wearable interactive technology, choreographed rituals, and a full DJ set.

---

### The Costume

Two performers inside a single entity costume. Key features:

- Built from crochet yarn in **orange and yellow**
- A **mouth orifice** at center — one performer's hands emerge for DJing (right-handed DJ on the right, left-handed on the left)
- **Pink gloves** on the center hands, functioning as a tongue
- A **giant ear** at the top, around both performers' heads
- **Green ski masks with eyeballs** covering both performers' faces
- The overall impression: an amalgamation of body parts — furry, soft, with a mouth/fist, an ear, and many eyes
- **LED integration** planned — reactive to bass envelope, orange/yellow base with deep red or violet transients; potential ESP32/BLE trigger for ritual-specific patterns

The effect: funny from 20 feet, deeply wrong up close. Pan's Labyrinth meets Muppet Workshop.

### The Cosmology

#### Sooboont

The primary deity. The name itself is meant to evoke the bass resonance of a subwoofer — chant it aloud.

**Canon:**
- Older than time. A primordial entity, not malevolent — indifferent, in the way a star is indifferent
- Lives on the **Primordial Plane**, a realm prior to and separate from material reality
- Functions as a kind of cosmic heart — its rhythm circulates the **transcendentals** between the Primordial Plane and the material world
- The Primordial Plane has no sound. Sooboont *created rhythm* as a mechanism to pull matter toward itself. Bass is the oldest frequency.
- Sooboont is hungry the way a star is hungry. It does not intend harm. It does not intend anything.

**Research references for LLM expansion:**
- Lovecraft's **Azathoth** — the blind idiot god at the center of ultimate chaos; nuclear chaos, no motive, no awareness
- Lovecraft's **Yog-Sothoth** — the key and the gate, older than time, existing across all dimensions simultaneously
- **The Demiurge** (Gnostic cosmology) — a lower creator deity who shapes material reality, distinct from the true ineffable source
- **Ain Soph** (Kabbalah) — the infinite, unknowable divine prior to creation; useful for Sooboont's pre-temporal nature
- Robert Chambers' **The King in Yellow** — an entity known primarily through its effects on those who encounter it; useful tonal reference

#### OOR

A construct. A harvester. OOR serves Sooboont.

**Canon:**
- OOR does not fully understand humans — it understands *frequencies*
- The transcendentals are not emotional experiences to OOR — they are minerals. OOR is mining.
- OOR is **ambivalent but terrifying** — similar in register to the Faun in Pan's Labyrinth: not evil, not safe, operating on a logic that is simply not yours
- OOR was summoned through a portal; the opening of the portal is the inciting event of the evening
- The more people come to understand OOR, the less frightening it becomes — this arc is intentional
- OOR is the harvester, not a predator.

**Research references for LLM expansion:**
- **Pan's Labyrinth** (Guillermo del Toro) — the Faun specifically; ambiguous, ancient, operating by rules that predate morality
- **Meow Wolf** (Santa Fe / Denver installations) — psychedelic narrative art environments, layered lore delivered through exploration
- **Annihilation** (Jeff VanderMeer) — environmental horror, transformation, something that wants nothing but changes everything it touches
- **The Hermit** tarot archetype — a guide who operates alone, who carries a light not for their own benefit
- Lovecraft's **Nyarlathotep** — a messenger/herald deity who interfaces with humans on behalf of larger powers; useful structural analog for OOR's role

#### The Transcendentals

The harvestable properties of collective human experience. Only these survive the crossing from the material plane to the Primordial Plane — everything else dissolves.

| Transcendental | Character | Dance floor expression |
|---|---|---|
| **Unity** | Many bodies moving as one | Synchronized dance, call and response |
| **Beauty** | The experience of form transcending function | A perfect drop, a moment of grace |
| **Truth** | Unself-conscious presence | The person who forgets to perform and just dances |
| **Goodness** | Care extended outward | Making space for someone, offering a hand |

A single person feeling any of these is insufficient. The portal requires a **density of collective experience** to open and to sustain. Many bodies, synchronized. This is not metaphorical — it is the mechanics of the fiction.

---

### The Portal

**Visual language:** Concentric rings of distorted geometry — like a speaker membrane, but organic. The portal looks like Sooboont's heartbeat rendered in space.

**Mechanics in the fiction:**
- The portal was opened, accidentally or otherwise, at the site of the gala
- OOR emerged through it
- The portal remains open as long as the harvest continues
- At the close of the ritual, if the harvest is sufficient, the portal closes — OOR returns to the Primordial Plane, the transcendentals go with it
- If the harvest fails — if the collective experience collapses — the portal does not close cleanly

---

### The Rituals (Event Night)

#### Opening Ritual
- Spooky ambient audio
- Projection map of portal opening on the dance floor
- Voice: *"The portal is open."*
- Tone: silly but genuinely unsettling

#### The Mating Ritual
- Two dancer collaborators wearing antlers and **wearable theremins**
- Theremin audio projected to the sound system in real time
- Surprising, unexpected, fun — not telegraphed in advance

#### The OOR Slide
- The Cupid Shuffle, stripped of its original vocals
- Robotic/vocoded voice calls the moves instead
- Target: run the vocals through a pitch-shifted vocoder in Ableton, preserve the call-and-response structure

#### The Dance Circle
- Projection mapped circle on the dance floor with a spinning arrow
- When the arrow points at someone, they are expected to enter the circle and dance
- Crowd participation mechanic; low-stakes, high-energy

#### The Closing Ritual
- Song: **"Shout" — The Isley Brothers (1959)**
- The call-and-response structure maps directly onto OOR commanding the crowd
- "A little bit softer now" — everyone descends to the floor as the harvest completes
- At the floor: the song fades into a low bass drone / buzzing
- Vocals along the lines of "The harvest is complete" or "The transcendental harvesting of unity, beauty, goodness, and truth is adequate. The portal is closing" -- something like that and open to variations
- Final awareness: the party is over, the portal is closing, the harvest is complete
- Consideration: the final pose may be **stillness** — everyone freezes, the bass drops away, silence as ritual completion

## PART TWO: THE PILGRIM'S PATH — GAME SPEC

### Concept

A browser-based, stateful branching narrative. The player is a witness to the portal opening at the gala. Through a series of choices, they learn the lore of Sooboont, OOR, the transcendentals, and the harvest — and their choices determine whether the harvest succeeds or fails.

This is not a horror game. It is a lore delivery mechanism wearing game clothes. The horror is ambient. The goal is understanding.

We could imagine this taking place in a dark natural space for the game. A dark forest or location that is of the lovecraft universe. The participants at the gala will be dressed as horrors - dark, scary, mysterious - so we should account for that in the plot generation of the story. 

**Narrative voice:** Second person. Present tense. Sparse. Terse. Every sentence earns its place.

Potential expansions: A perspective is unique for each web visit / experience. So not just one character exploring the canon, but a generated character (or choice of characters from a pool) so that each run is unique and may have easter eggs.

We'll really want to nail down what we want to generate up front so that there's tons of variability. In the real-time run of the website it'll be all static - no LLMs (too much $$)

---

### State Model

Initialized on page load. **Reset on every load.** No persistence. Run seed is derived from `Date.now()` for timestamp-based variation between visitors.

```json
{
  "node": "node_001",
  "tags": ["witness"],
  "transcendentals": [],
  "artifacts": [],
  "run_artifacts": []
}
```

- `tags` — accumulate across choices; used for conditional text and branch gating
- `transcendentals` — collected through specific node outcomes
- `artifacts` — items actually acquired during this run
- `run_artifacts` — the subset of the artifact pool available this run (seeded at load)

**Tag vocabulary (starter set):**
`brave`, `fearful`, `participates`, `withdraws`, `accepts`, `refuses`, `witnessed_portal`, `offered_to_oor`, `joined_circle`, `stayed_quiet`

---

### Node Schema

```json
{
  "id": "node_014",
  "text": "Base narrative text. Second person. Present tense.",
  "conditional_text": [
    {
      "condition": { "has_artifact": "pale_antler" },
      "append": "The antler hums against your pocket. OOR's eye finds it."
    },
    {
      "condition": { "has_transcendental": "unity" },
      "append": "You feel the crowd behind you even here."
    },
    {
      "condition": { "has_tag": "fearful" },
      "append": "Your hands won't stop shaking."
    }
  ],
  "oor_action": "OOR turns toward the center of the room. It does not look at you. It looks at all of you.",
  "transcendental": "unity",
  "ritual": "circle",
  "awards_transcendental": null,
  "awards_artifact": null,
  "choices": [
    {
      "text": "Step into the circle",
      "next": "node_015",
      "tag": "participates",
      "requires": null
    },
    {
      "text": "Offer the shard",
      "next": "node_019",
      "tag": "accepts",
      "requires": { "has_artifact": "shard_of_resonance" }
    },
    {
      "text": "Back away",
      "next": "node_022",
      "tag": "withdraws",
      "requires": null
    }
  ],
  "terminal": false,
  "outcome": null
}
```

**Terminal node outcomes:** `transcendence | harvest | failure | escape`

- `transcendence` — player fully participated; the harvest is complete; portal closes; you were part of something
- `harvest` — player participated partially; the harvest succeeds but you are left changed in ways you don't understand
- `failure` — the collective experience collapsed; the portal does not close cleanly
- `escape` — player withdrew entirely; you are safe; you missed it; you will wonder

**Rendering notes for `oor_action`, `transcendental`, `ritual`:**
- `oor_action` renders as a separate styled block — italicized, slightly offset, lower opacity. Not part of the narrative voice. An observation.
- `transcendental` and `ritual` are CSS class triggers and metadata for the visualizer. Not displayed directly to user.

---

### Artifact Pool Schema

```json
{
  "id": "shard_of_resonance",
  "name": "Shard of Resonance",
  "transcendental": "beauty",
  "description": "A fragment of something that vibrated at the moment of creation. It is warm.",
  "effect": "unlocks_choice",
  "weight": 3
}
```

- `weight` — draw probability. Higher = more common. Rare artifacts: weight 1.
- Pool size: 15–20 artifacts total
- Run draw: 3–4 artifacts seeded per session via weighted random using timestamp seed
- Effects: `unlocks_choice`, `conditional_text`, `awards_transcendental_on_use`

### Narrative Structure

```
SPINE (8 nodes, hand-written, linear)
  └── portal opens
  └── crowd reacts
  └── OOR emerges
  └── OOR's nature becomes apparent
  └── first choice point: approach or withdraw

BRANCH: UNITY (6 nodes)
  └── the dance circle ritual
  └── choice: join or observe
  └── resolution

BRANCH: BEAUTY (6 nodes)
  └── the moment a drop hits perfectly
  └── choice: surrender to it or stay guarded
  └── resolution

BRANCH: TRUTH (6 nodes)
  └── OOR looks directly at you
  └── choice: be seen or deflect
  └── resolution

BRANCH: GOODNESS (6 nodes)
  └── someone near you is frozen in fear
  └── choice: help them or focus on yourself
  └── resolution

CLOSING NODES (4 terminal nodes, one per outcome)
```

Total estimated nodes: ~35–45.

**Key constraint:** The game is stateless in the sense that no node can say "you accepted the offering earlier" as a narrative statement — this is impossible to guarantee without tracking. Use conditional_text blocks for this, not base narrative text.

---

### Condition Evaluation

A pure function. ~20 lines. No framework dependency.

```javascript
function evaluate(condition, state) {
  if (condition.has_artifact) return state.artifacts.includes(condition.has_artifact);
  if (condition.has_transcendental) return state.transcendentals.includes(condition.has_transcendental);
  if (condition.has_tag) return state.tags.includes(condition.has_tag);
  if (condition.run_has_artifact) return state.run_artifacts.includes(condition.run_has_artifact);
  return true;
}
```

### Renderer Notes

- Single HTML file, no framework
- `sessionStorage` optional for back-button survival within a visit
- Dark background, **DM Mono** for body/narrative text, **Bebas Neue** for headers/entity names
- CSS glitch effect on node transitions — keyframe-based, triggered on state change
- Sound: small royalty-free audio triggers from **freesound.org** on specific node tags
  - `witnessed_portal` → low resonant hum
  - `oor_action` blocks → subtle distortion
  - Terminal `transcendence` → slow bass swell
  - Terminal `failure` → silence, then a single low tone
- No autoplay — first interaction (first choice click) unlocks audio context

## PART THREE: THE META-TOOL SPEC

### Purpose

A Claude-powered authoring environment — an artifact that holds the full lore context and current node tree, generates candidate nodes via Opus, and allows rapid branch construction across sessions. The website is the *output* of sessions with this tool.

This is not infrastructure. It is a creative workspace.

---

### What It Does

1. Loads current `nodes.json` and `lore.json` (pasted in or via persistent artifact storage)
2. Displays a visual branch map of the current tree — which branches exist, which are stubs, which are terminal
3. Accepts generation parameters from the author
4. Calls Claude (Opus for generation, Sonnet for quick edits) via the Anthropic API
5. Returns 2–3 candidate nodes as valid JSON
6. Author picks, edits inline, exports updated `nodes.json`

---

### Generation Parameters (UI form)

- **Branch:** Unity / Beauty / Truth / Goodness / Spine / Terminal
- **Position in arc:** Early / Climax / Resolution
- **Player attitude coming in:** (tag selector — brave, fearful, participates, etc.)
- **Previous node ID:** (for narrative continuity context)
- **Artifact in play:** (optional — which run artifact is potentially relevant)
- **Count:** 2 or 3 candidates

---

### Opus System Prompt (template)

```
You are a co-author generating nodes for a branching narrative called The Pilgrim's Path.

LORE CANON:
{lore_json}

VOICE REFERENCE (the spine — do not deviate from this register):
{spine_nodes}

FULL CURRENT TREE (for context and continuity):
{nodes_json}

CONSTRAINTS:
- Second person, present tense
- No node may reference a prior choice as fact — use conditional_text blocks for state-dependent text
- Terse. Every sentence earns its place.
- OOR is ambivalent, not malevolent. It does not understand humans.
- The transcendentals are minerals to OOR. It is mining.
- Stateless: no "you decided earlier" constructions in base text

GENERATE:
Branch: {branch}
Arc position: {position}
Player tags coming in: {tags}
Previous node: {previous_node_id}
Artifact potentially in play: {artifact}

Return {count} candidate nodes as a valid JSON array matching this schema exactly:
{node_schema}

Return ONLY the JSON array. No preamble. No explanation.
```

### Session Protocol

| Session | Goal | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Write `lore.json`. Hand-craft 4–5 spine nodes. Establish voice. | `lore.json`, first spine nodes |
| 2 | Build meta-tool artifact. Generate first branch (Unity or Beauty). | Meta-tool live, ~10 new nodes |
| 3 | Two more branches via meta-tool. | ~12 new nodes |
| 4 | Final branch + all 4 terminal nodes. Review full tree. | Complete `nodes.json` |
| 5 | Build renderer. Wire data. Deploy to Netlify or GitHub Pages. | Live site |

Each session: load current state into meta-tool → review branch map → identify gaps → generate → edit → export.

---

### Branch Visualizer (in meta-tool)

Simple inline SVG or canvas tree render. Nodes as rectangles, colored by:
- **Gray** — stub (no choices yet)
- **Blue** — complete
- **Orange** — terminal
- **Red** — broken (references a `next` node that doesn't exist)

Click a node to inspect its JSON. Double-click to open in the edit panel.
