# The Evening — Timeline Document
**What happens in the room. Not what anyone sees — what occurs.**
**Perspective-agnostic. Characters layer on top of this.**

---

## Before the Party

The portal has not opened yet. The warehouse is a warehouse. The dance floor is concrete. The equipment is set up — decks, PA, fog machines, projection rig aimed at the floor. The antlered companions are present, dressed like anyone else. They are waiting. They may not know they are waiting.

DJ OOR is behind the decks. The lights are low enough and the fog thick enough that no one has gotten a clear look. From across the room, in the dark, it could be a person in a costume. It is not a person in a costume.

---

## The Opening — The Portal Activates

The first bass note is not music. It is the portal opening.

The dance floor becomes the aperture — the physical connection between the Primordial Plane and the material world. The concrete is still concrete. The projection rig casts concentric rings onto it — distorted geometry, organic, pulsing. The floor vibrates from underneath. Not from the subwoofers. From below.

The call goes out. It travels through the ground, through the walls, through the infrastructure of the city. It is subconscious. It is felt in the sternum, in the teeth, in the soles of the feet. People within range are redirected. They were going somewhere else. They cannot remember where. They follow the pressure gradient toward the warehouse. They think they heard about a party.

The transcendental flow between the Primordial Plane and the material world has been blocked. Clogged. The portal is the intervention — a drain being cleared. The crowd, once assembled on the aperture, will become the conduit. Their collective experience will be the circuit through which the transcendentals pass.

This is not theft. This is maintenance. The distinction does not make it less terrifying.

---

## Early Set — The Room Fills

DJ OOR is operating. The music is good — genuinely good. Bass-heavy, dark, danceable. People arrive and start moving. The fog is thick. The lights pulse in orange and violet. The projection on the floor shifts slowly — the rings expand and contract like breathing.

The room fills. Bodies accumulate on the portal. The dance floor becomes dense. Individual movement begins to soften at the edges — not synchronized yet, but trending. The boundary between people is loosening.

DJ OOR is visible in fragments. A flash of orange in the fog. Pink gloves catching the light. The shape behind the decks does not resolve into a person. The ear is visible if you look up, but the fog and the dark make it easy to mistake for rigging or decoration. The eyes — the green, misaligned eyes — are the thing that catches people. One person sees them and looks away. Another sees them and cannot.

The temperature on the dance floor is warm and getting warmer. The edges of the room are cold. This is not HVAC. This is proximity to the mechanism.

The antlered companions are dancing. They look like guests. They are inside the crowd, feeling the room, waiting for activation.

---

## The DJ OOR Slide — Synchronization Primer

The music shifts. A familiar song — recognizable structure, call-and-response, directional movement — but filtered through something wrong. The vocals are vocoded, robotic, arriving from a direction that doesn't match the speaker placement. The song is the same and it is not the same. It has been pulled through the portal and come back changed.

The crowd follows. They know the moves. Left, right, slide. The calls come and the bodies respond. For the first time tonight, many people move as one. The synchronization is not voluntary — it is induced by the structure of the song, by the bass pattern, by something underneath both that the body responds to before the mind can object.

Unity floods the aperture. The portal brightens. The projection rings accelerate. DJ OOR makes an adjustment on the low end. The harvest has begun.

The comedy is real. People are laughing. They are having fun. This is also real. The harvest does not require suffering. It requires density. Joy works. Laughter works. Moving together works. DJ OOR does not understand why the slide works. It works.

---

## The Mating Ritual — Activation and Selection

The music shifts again. Darker. More textured. Whimsical and spooky simultaneously — melodic lines that curl and retreat, bass that breathes instead of pounds. Something has changed in the room and the crowd feels it before they can name it.

The antlered companions activate. The transition is not dramatic — they were dancing, and now they are doing something else. Antlers appear. One of them is carrying the divining rod of Sooboont. It was not visible a moment ago. It is visible now.

The familiar with the divining rod moves through the crowd. The rod resonates in unison with the transcendent force inside each person she approaches — a sound that is part of the music and also separate from it, a harmonic that exists between the notes. She is not choosing people. The rod is identifying them. She is the instrument. The intention comes from elsewhere.

The second familiar choreographs alongside her. Together they are one unit — measurement and direction. People are selected and paired. The pairing is not romantic. It is harmonic. Two people whose transcendental frequencies complement each other are brought into proximity. The effect is immediate: self-consciousness dissolves. The paired individuals stop performing and start moving. The boundary between them softens.

This happens across the floor simultaneously. Multiple pairings. Multiple dissolutions. The room's individualism is breaking down — not violently, not even noticeably to most. It looks like the party getting better. It looks like people loosening up. It looks like the moment at a wedding when everyone stops being polite and starts dancing for real.

From outside, if you could see the whole room at once, you would see the resonance patterns forming. Harmonics increasing. Nodes of density where the transcendental concentration is highest. The portal responds — the projection rings pulse faster, brighter. The floor is warm.

The ritual ends in group celebration. Not a defined ending — a dissolve. The pairings merge into clusters, the clusters merge into the crowd, and the crowd is different now. Less individual. More unified. The Mating Ritual did not create this. It accelerated what was already happening.

---

## The Dance Circle — Final Extraction

The projection changes. The concentric rings contract to a single circle of light on the dance floor. Inside it, a spinning arrow.

This is the last act of extraction before the closing. The arrow is not random — it is directed by DJ OOR, by the resonance patterns in the room, by the divining rod's data. It finds the people who are closest to full transcendental yield. The people who have been broken open by the night. The people who have the most to give.

When the arrow points at you, you are expected to enter the circle. The crowd watches. The crowd wants you to enter. The crowd's wanting has weight — it is not social pressure, it is the gravitational pull of a unified field. Someone always enters.

Inside the circle, you are visible. You are seen by two hundred people and by DJ OOR and by whatever is on the other side of the aperture. The exposure is the mechanism. Truth — unselfconscious presence — is extracted in the circle. The person who enters and dances without performing, without watching themselves, without audience — that person yields the purest truth the harvest will collect tonight.

The circle rotates through several people. Each one enters, dances, yields, exits. The crowd absorbs them back. The transcendental density in the room is approaching critical threshold.

---

## The Closing — The Harvest Completes

DJ OOR knows. The mechanism has reached sufficiency. The transcendental flow between the Primordial Plane and the material world is restored. The clog is clearing. The drain is open.

The music shifts one final time. Call and response. The crowd is fully synchronized now — a single organism responding to DJ OOR's commands. Lower. Lower. The crowd descends. Bodies bend, kneel, sink toward the floor. The floor that is the portal. The floor that has been the aperture all night.

At the floor, the music fades to a low bass drone. The vibration from underneath meets the vibration from above. For one moment, the crowd is the thinnest membrane between two planes. The transcendentals pass through them — unity, beauty, truth, goodness — flowing from the material world through the conduit of two hundred bodies into the Primordial Plane. The circulation is restored.

Then: stillness. The bass drops away. Silence.

The portal closes. The projection fades. The dance floor is concrete again. DJ OOR is gone — or DJ OOR is still behind the decks, inert, a costume on a stand, a shape that no longer moves. The fog begins to clear. The lights come up slowly. The room is a warehouse again.

The crowd stands. They are dazed. They are lighter. Some of them are smiling. Some of them are crying. None of them can explain why. They collect their coats. They walk to their cars. The cold outside is specific and familiar. They drive home.

Something has been taken from them. Something has been restored to the world because of them. These are the same event. They will not understand this. They will remember the bass, the heat, the feeling of being part of something. They will not remember what.

---

## After

The warehouse is empty. The equipment is still set up. The fog machines are off. The projection rig is dark.

The alley outside is the same length it was before.

If the harvest succeeded.
