14 Vocoder Wash 118 BPM
No 303. Just a massive vocoder chord pad — six voices cycling through vowel formants — over a kick and snare. The pad IS the song. Formants sweep slowly, one per bar. What the reversed shuffle sounds like if you hold it still.

15 The Full Stack 118 BPM
The build. Every 2 bars a new layer enters: kick → hats + bass → acid opens → vocoder pad → melody → vocoder voice. At bar 13 the beat drops out. At bar 15 everything returns. At bar 17 it deconstructs — layers exit one by one until only the chamfer drone remains. ~36 seconds.

16 Higher State 126 BPM
Josh Wink territory. One 303 pattern, one macro filter sweep that opens across 48 seconds. The filter starts closed (200Hz) and crawls to 5kHz with the Q rising from 12 to 22. A vocoder hook — "higher, higher" — enters at the midpoint and rides the filter up. Delay effect on the 303. The whole track is one long crescendo. ~48 seconds.

17 Slow Ascent 120 BPM
Pure acid, no voices. The Higher State filter mechanic applied to a simpler pattern — one macro sweep from 200Hz to 4kHz over 32 seconds. Q rises from 10 to 18. Dotted-eighth delay fades in at the midpoint. Sawtooth wave for warmth. The filter IS the composition. ~32 seconds.

18 Tech Ritual 124 BPM
Tech house intro — 4 bars of drums only: tight kick, clap on 2 and 4, closed hats on eighths, shaker on sixteenths. Then the 303 creeps in with the filter at 200Hz and sweeps to 3500Hz over 12 bars. Dotted-eighth delay. Sawtooth. The groove establishes before the acid takes over. ~32 seconds.

19 Deep Offering 124 BPM
The masterpiece. F minor. Bars 1-4: tech house drums. Bars 5-8: 303 acid enters. Bars 9-12: a rolling bass house bassline drops in — sawtooth through a musical filter, sidechain-pumping against the kick, following Fm→Ab→Bb→Fm. A sine topline melody enters with the bass. Bar 17: the beat drops out, bass and melody ring alone. Bar 19: everything crashes back. ~48 seconds of bass house meets acid.
