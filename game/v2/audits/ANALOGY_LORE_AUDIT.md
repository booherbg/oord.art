# Analogy & Lore Consistency Audit

Every analogy, simile, and metaphor across all Wanderer and Familiar nodes examined for: Does it actually mean something? Is it the right register? Does it try too hard? Cross-referenced against lore details in Run 1 and Run 2 (final draft) for inconsistencies.

---

## Analogy Flags — Wanderer Nodes

### HARD FLAGS — Broken or wrong. Fix these.

**w_004 conditional_text (no condition, always shows):**
> "Your instinct was right. The wrongness was not just the place itself. It was the purpose that this place is being put to."

The Wanderer hasn't learned anything about "purpose" yet. They just saw a tentacled DJ. This is the game telling the player something the character doesn't know. Authorial intrusion. The conditional has no condition field so it fires for every player.

**Fix:** Either add a condition (`has_tag: saw_oor`) so it only fires for players who leaned in and watched, or rewrite to stay with what the Wanderer actually perceives: "Your stomach is tight but you don't look away. Whatever is behind those decks, it isn't performing."

---

**w_008e (THE MATING RITUAL):**
> "It looks like something that was here long before we crawled out of the dark and started moving together. Something ancient and patient. We just happen to be what it works with."

Three "it looks like" constructions in sequence. First two are concrete (party getting looser, people finding their groove). Third leaps to evolutionary cosmology. The Wanderer doesn't think in "we crawled out of the dark" terms — that's omniscient narration about human origins. "We just happen to be what it works with" is a thesis statement about the game's cosmology. The pattern setup → payoff is the author being clever, not the character being observant.

**Fix:** Cut the third "it looks like" and the sentence after it. End on "It looks like people finding their groove." The reader sees what you see. They don't need the cosmic interpretation.

---

**w_010a (INSIDE THE DANCE CIRCLE):**
> "feeding you something that tastes like permission"

What does permission taste like? This is the "sounds poetic but doesn't map" category. The intention is clear (the crowd gives you permission to be visible) but "tastes" doesn't connect to "permission." The synesthesia doesn't produce an image, just a nice-sounding phrase.

**Fix:** "feeding you something that feels like permission" — simpler, works. Or cut the taste entirely: "cheering, stomping, and the sound is permission."

---

**w_slide_run2b (WRONG WAY):**
> "Not because the frequency aligned you. Because he reached out and you accepted it."

The Wanderer shouldn't use "frequency." Voice rules: the Wanderer feels "vibration/hum/warmth," not "frequency." Frequency is the Familiar's word.

**Fix:** "Not because the music moved you together. Because he reached out and you accepted it." Or: "Not because the room pushed you toward each other."

---

**w_012 conditional (beauty tag):**
> "The Builder's signature, printed on card stock"

The Wanderer doesn't use "the Builder" as a title. In the Run 4 nodes, they think "Someone built this" — they observe a person, not a role. "The Builder" is lore terminology.

**Fix:** "A signature, printed on card stock" or "Their signature."

---

**w_beauty_yielded (THE MAKING):**
> "Not the bartender's calibration."

"Calibration" is Builder language (measurement, engineering). The Wanderer would perceive the bartender's attention differently.

**Fix:** "Not the bartender's quiet study." Or: "Not the bartender's careful watching."

---

**w_beauty_yielded (THE MAKING):**
> "adjust a send level"

"Send level" is audio engineering terminology. The Wanderer doesn't know signal routing. They see hands on equipment.

**Fix:** "reach for the next knob" or "adjust something on the rack."

---

**w_011 (THE CLOSING RITUAL):**
> "You are the conduit."

"Conduit" has specific lore meaning in this game (the circle makes a person "a conduit" per the Familiar's description). The Wanderer wouldn't reach for this technical term. They'd feel "something passing through."

**Fix:** "Something is passing through you." Cut the label, keep the sensation.

---

**w_003_rings (THE RINGS):**
> "As you continue to stare into this unholy aperture"

"Aperture" is a lore term. The Wanderer would say "hole" or "opening" or "whatever this is." Also "unholy" is a moral judgment — the Wanderer tends toward physical description rather than moral categorization.

**Fix:** "As you continue to stare into this opening" or "into whatever this is."

### SOFT FLAGS — Borderline. Consider fixing.

**w_002:**
> "a warehouse, something with the bones of industrial purpose"

"Bones of industrial purpose" is a slightly reaching metaphor. The building has an industrial frame. "Bones" for structure works, but "of industrial purpose" is abstract. It's trying to make a warehouse sound more literary than it needs to.

**Consider:** "a warehouse, industrial, long-abandoned" — or just let it be a warehouse.

---

**w_003:**
> "The dance floor is melding but still emerging."

What does this mean physically? The crowd on the dance floor is melding (coming together) but still emerging (still forming)? "Melding" and "emerging" are both abstract verbs without a clear physical referent. This sentence doesn't produce an image.

**Consider:** "The dance floor is filling in. Not full yet, but getting there." Or cut it and let the next sentence (the hair-push synchronization) show the emergence.

---

**w_003:**
> "like soil after rain in a place where it hasn't rained in a very long time"

Petrichor (the smell of rain on dry earth) requires rain to have actually fallen. "Soil after rain" in a place without rain is paradoxical. The intention is uncanny (a smell that shouldn't be here), but the simile's own logic undermines it. If it hasn't rained, there's no petrichor.

**Consider:** "like soil that remembers rain" — preserves the uncanniness without the logical contradiction. Or: "sweet and old, like opening a door to a room that's been closed for years."

---

**w_003b (THE SECURITY GUARD):**
> "He's standing the way someone stands when they saw a picture of standing once and committed to it."

Funny. But it implies the Wanderer knows the bouncer learned standing from a picture, which is information the Wanderer can't have. This is actually the Familiar's perspective (the bouncer is a construct mimicking human behavior). The Wanderer can observe THAT something is off about the standing, but not WHY.

**Consider:** This line is good enough to keep. The Wanderer is allowed to make humorous observations about people. But it walks a fine line between observation and omniscience. If it bothers you, a safer version: "He stands the way someone stands when they've been standing a very long time without a reason to stop."

---

**w_004:**
> "adjusting knobs with the efficiency of someone threading a machine"

"Threading a machine" — which machine? Sewing machine? Film projector? Loom? The vagueness undermines the specificity the analogy is trying to achieve. The reader has to guess which type of threading.

**Consider:** "adjusting knobs the way someone threads a sewing machine — practiced, mechanical, not looking" — or pick a specific machine. Or just cut the simile: "adjusting knobs without looking down."

---

**w_004b:**
> "This is not a DJ. This is a technician."

"Technician" is categorization. The editorial rules say characters perceive, they don't categorize. "Not X, this is Y" is an analytical frame.

**Consider:** Cutting "This is a technician" and going straight to "You are standing inside the instrument it is playing." The conclusion carries more weight without the label.

---

**w_008f (THE GAP BETWEEN):**
> "The crowd's grief is in you now. Like a rib."

What does grief share with a rib? A rib is structural, ever-present, part of your frame. Is grief structural? Ever-present? The mapping is loose. The simile evokes something — grief as load-bearing, something you don't notice until it breaks — but the connection requires more work from the reader than the payoff justifies.

**Consider:** "The crowd's grief is in you now. Part of the frame." — less poetic, more physical. Or: "The crowd's grief is in you now. You can feel it the way you feel your own breathing — always there, only noticeable when you notice it."

---

**w_008f arrival variant:**
> "the thinking part of you is still here but it has gone quiet the way a monkey stops reaching when it finally has something to hold"

The monkey metaphor. It's vivid and it maps well (the restless mind calming when it grasps something). But "monkey" feels imported from a meditation tradition (monkey mind is a Buddhist concept). The Wanderer's register is church basements and car rides, not dharma talks.

**Consider:** If "monkey" feels too borrowed: "the thinking part of you is still here but it has gone quiet the way a dog stops pacing when the car stops." Domestic. Lived experience.

---

**w_slide_run2b:**
> "It feels grating, like rubbing reptile scales the wrong way or trying to push two magnets together in the wrong alignment"

Two similes for one sensation. Pick one. The magnet one is more universally experienced.

**Consider:** Cut "rubbing reptile scales the wrong way." Keep "like trying to push two magnets together wrong."

---

**w_slide_run2b:**
> "something between comfort and hubris"

The Wanderer is doing psychology on themselves. "Between comfort and hubris" is analytical — naming and categorizing the emotional cocktail that made them go left instead of right. The Wanderer feels this, doesn't diagnose it.

**Consider:** "You were so sure. Your feet were sure. Your feet were wrong."

---

**w_bartender_run2_drink (whiskey variant):**
> "Like deja vu, but without the doubt."

Deja vu IS doubt. It's defined by the uncanny sense that you might have experienced something before but can't be sure. Remove the doubt and you have... memory. Or recognition. The phrase contradicts itself.

**Consider:** "Like remembering something you've never done." Or: "Not quite deja vu. Stronger than that. Your body remembers this drink."

---

**w_slide_run2a:**
> "two hundred bodies locked into the same synchronicity"

"Synchronization" earlier in the node and "synchronicity" here. Redundant. Also "synchronicity" (Jungian meaningful coincidence) means something different from "synchronization" (moving at the same time). The wrong word.

**Consider:** "two hundred bodies locked into the same rhythm."

---

**w_003_rings:**
> "you can feel a bubbling sense of vertigo"

Vertigo doesn't bubble. "Bubbling" is a liquid verb applied to a spatial disorientation. Unusual and unproductive synesthesia.

**Consider:** "a rising vertigo" or "vertigo pulling at you."

---

**w_behind_the_rack:**
> "The bass is so deep it's not sound anymore. It's the floor disagreeing with itself."

Floors don't disagree. This is anthropomorphism that doesn't map to a physical sensation. What does the Wanderer actually feel? Vibrations going different directions? Resonance patterns interfering?

**Consider:** "It's the floor vibrating in two directions at once." Or: "It's the floor trying to be in two places at once." Both more physical.

---

**w_011 (saw_oor conditional):**
> "remembering the experience you had earlier when bearing witness on the DJ-in-the-mix"

"Bearing witness on the DJ-in-the-mix" is awkward phrasing. "Bearing witness" is formal/ceremonial. "The DJ-in-the-mix" is a phrase that doesn't appear elsewhere. This reads like a sentence that was revised multiple times and never landed.

**Consider:** "remembering what you saw behind the mixer" — direct, clean.

## Lore Consistency Audit

### INCONSISTENCY: Gravel lot vs. paved lot

**w_002:** "The alley opens onto a gravel lot."
**w_loading_dock_insta:** The beanie girl says "The parking lot is gravel — it's paved now, or was when you walked in."

The Wanderer walks across a gravel lot in w_002 (all runs). Then in Run 3, an NPC says the lot is now paved. Direct contradiction within the same playthrough.

**Fix:** Either change w_002 to "a paved lot" (and update w_exit_001 which also says "gravel lot"), or change the instagram dialogue to say the lot in the OLD photo was paved and it's gravel now (implying the building has degraded, which fits the uncanny tone). The second option is more interesting: the photo shows a nicer version of the building that predates the current crumbling one, adding to the wrongness.

---

### INCONSISTENCY: w_011 conditional text — OOR present AND gone

**w_011 conditional (saw_oor):** Describes OOR as still present but mundane in the overhead light. "frilly crochet yarn, hot-glued and matted."
**w_011 conditional (entered_circle):** "DJ OOR is gone. All that remains are two green scraps of fabric."

A player can have BOTH tags (saw_oor from w_004b, entered_circle from w_010a). Both conditionals fire additively (no `conditional_mode: first_match` on this node). The player reads OOR is present and mundane, THEN reads OOR is gone. Contradiction.

**Fix:** Add `conditional_mode: "first_match"` to w_011's conditional_text. Or add mutually exclusive conditions. Or rewrite the saw_oor text to be compatible: "You remember what you saw behind the mixer earlier. The fog has cleared now. The surface that seemed alive..." (past tense for the observation, not present — works whether OOR is still there or gone).

---

### SOFT INCONSISTENCY: Bouncer competence

**f_002:** The bouncer "does not know what security means in this context." He chose the jacket because it was near the door.
**w_bouncer_run2:** The bouncer asks for "cash or equivalent," examines the yarn with "careful study," pulls snips from his pocket, clips a piece, accepts it as payment.

The Familiar says the bouncer doesn't understand his role. But in Run 2, the bouncer performs a complex artifact assessment (identifying yarn as valid currency, having specialized tools). These are different levels of competence.

**Possible resolution:** The familiar constructs don't understand HUMAN concepts (security, payment) but do understand operational functions (checking for artifacts is a gate function, not a security function). The bouncer doesn't know what "security" means but does know what "valid artifact" means. This works but could use one line of clarification in f_002 or in the story_design doc. Not urgent but worth noting.

### SOFT INCONSISTENCY: Temporal mechanics across runs

**w_012 (came_for_stillness):** "You will come back."
**w_012 (came_for_drink):** "It feels like it's waiting."
**w_bouncer_run2:** The Wanderer returns, has the yarn, doesn't remember getting it clearly.
**f_001:** "You have been here before. Not here. Not this building."

The runs are different nights/harvests (confirmed by the Familiar's "not this building"). But the Wanderer has deja vu and carries artifacts between runs. The bartender improves from Run 1 (ratios off) to Run 2 (ratios perfect), implying the familiar constructs learn across harvests.

This all works IF each harvest is a distinct event at a distinct venue, with the familiar constructs carrying knowledge between harvests. The Wanderer carries artifacts but not memories. The familiar constructs carry technique but not specific recall.

**Not a bug — but worth documenting.** The temporal mechanics should probably be made explicit in story_design.md so that future content stays consistent.
