# Wanderer Run 3 (Truth Branch) — Voice Audit
**Nodes reviewed: w_loading_dock, w_bartender_run3, w_silence_run3, w_jacket_woman**
**Voice rules applied from: feedback_wanderer_voice.md**

## w_loading_dock (THE LOADING DOCK)

**What's working:**
This is the strongest node in Run 3. Concrete, textured, funny. The dialogue is natural — "Huh" and "...hmm" do more work than any description of the characters' reactions could. The Instagram detail (3 followers, one called @left_pocket_empty) is an excellent Easter egg that rewards attentive players. "Never been to Fargo" is a perfect Coen Brothers wink. The matchbook returning to the pocket without anyone handing it back is good uncanny texture — grounded, physical, wrong.

> "Bass leaks through the gap — not the front-door bass, something rawer, less filtered. Like hearing a song through the wall of the room where it's being mixed."

This is a good analogy rooted in lived experience. Exactly right for the Wanderer.

**No flags.** This node is clean. Leave it alone.

## w_bartender_run3 (THE TUNING)

**What's working:**
The side-door entrance behind the bar is a great spatial detail — the loading dock route puts you somewhere you shouldn't be, and the wrongness is physical, not cosmic. The bartender's ceremony with the bottles ("poured in an order that seems more like ceremony than recipe") is good observation. The conditional text about denim jacket guy dancing is a nice callback.

**Flags:**

**1. Abstract filter metaphor:**
> "Something adjusts inside you. Not your mood. Something deeper — the lens you look through. The filter between you and the room. It gets thinner."

"Lens" and "filter" are Builder/systems language. The Wanderer doesn't think in signal processing terms. They feel this as a physical change. **Fix:** Replace with body sensation. Something like: "Something adjusts inside you. Not your mood. Deeper. Like blinking underwater and suddenly the water is clear — same room, same crowd, but the blur you didn't know was there is gone."

**2. Telling instead of showing:**
> "You didn't ask for this. She didn't offer it. It just happened, the way the yarn appeared in your pocket, the way the matchbook showed everyone something different. The mechanism does what it needs to do."

"The mechanism does what it needs to do" is straight-up Builder language. The Wanderer does not know about mechanisms. They feel the pattern without naming the system. **Fix:** Cut the last sentence entirely. The two preceding callbacks (yarn, matchbook) already establish the pattern. Let the reader make the connection.

**3. Borderline abstraction:**
> "It tastes like the moment right before you remember something important. Not the memory itself — the threshold. The tip-of-the-tongue feeling, sustained, stretched across your whole body."

This is a strong metaphor that mostly works. "Sustained" is slightly clinical — the word is doing analytical work ("this sensation is being extended in duration") rather than physical work. **Fix:** Try: "The tip-of-the-tongue feeling, drawn out, stretched across your whole body." "Drawn out" has more physical pull.

**4. "More specific" is vague:**
> "The room is the same room but you are seeing it differently. The edges are sharper. The fog is less opaque. The people are more specific."

"Edges sharper" and "fog less opaque" are good concrete observations. "The people are more specific" tries to be evocative but doesn't land — it's a summary of a sensation rather than the sensation itself. **Fix:** Show one person being more specific. "The room is the same room but you are seeing it differently. The edges are sharper. The fog is less opaque. The woman near the speakers — you can see the tendons in her wrist as she moves."

## w_silence_run3 (THE SILENCE)

**What's working:**
The concept is ambitious and it mostly delivers. The italicized inner-narrator voice (in the prose doc) going quiet is a powerful structural idea — the style change IS the content. The transition from ornate observation to simple declarative sentences after "goes quiet" works. The specific sensory details scattered through the observation phase (condensation on cups, fog on shoulders) keep it grounded. The jacket woman's arm-uncrossing is the best character moment in the entire run — "the way you might set down a bag you forgot you were carrying."

**Flags:**

**1. Systems language for the jacket woman:**
> "She has built a structure around herself and she is living inside it."

"Built a structure" is abstraction. The Wanderer sees surfaces, not architectures. Her jacket IS the showing — you don't need to name it a "structure." **Fix:** "She is wearing the jacket like armor. Zipped. Arms crossed. She has decided to be here and decided not to arrive."

**2. Telling the reader what the Wanderer knows:**
> "You know this because you do the same thing. You watch. You collect. You narrate the experience instead of having it."

This is narrating self-reflection, which the voice rules explicitly prohibit ("Don't narrate reflection"). The Wanderer recognizes the jacket woman physically, through the body — not through a thesis statement about their shared psychology. **Fix:** Cut "You know this because you do the same thing." Instead, show the recognition physically: "Your jaw tightens. You recognize the posture."

**3. The gap passage is philosophy, not observation:**
> "The gap is so full that the voice was the thing making it seem empty — filling the silence with description so you wouldn't notice that the silence was already complete."

This is a thesis statement. It's well-constructed and thematically crucial, but it's the Wanderer doing philosophy, which violates "Never reach for philosophy." The problem is this is the climax of the node — cutting it kills the beat. **Suggested fix:** Shorten. Don't explain the gap. Just show it: "The gap is not empty. The gap is the fullest thing you have ever felt. The voice was in the way."

**4. Meta-narration about narration risks circularity:**
> "You notice these things because the voice tells you to notice them. Or you notice them and the voice claims them. You're not sure anymore which comes first."

This is three sentences of the Wanderer analyzing their own cognitive process. It's the thematic engine of the node, so it can't be cut entirely, but it's dangerously close to "narrating reflection." One sentence would land harder than three. **Fix:** "You're not sure anymore whether you notice things or the voice tells you to."

**5. OOR action uses lore terminology:**
> "The aperture registers a new flow. The construct did not initiate this one."

This is fine — OOR action blocks are external/clinical, not player-voice. No issue.

**Structural note:** This node is ~380 words — significantly longer than the other Run 3 nodes (~200 words each). The length is justified by the weight of the moment, but it could lose 60-80 words by cutting the flagged redundancies and the prose would hit harder. The silence should feel like compression, not expansion.

## w_jacket_woman (THE MIRROR)

**What's working:**
The conversation is earned. After the silence node stripped the narrator, the dialogue feels genuine — two people talking without performance. "How long have you been coming here?" is perfectly banal. Her speech about watching and collecting and proving she was here is the mirror moment landing. "You are not documenting it. You are inside it" is the payoff.

The sub-floor response ("a shift in pressure, the way an ocean shifts when a river reaches it") is poetic. The conditional text about the truth_shard appearing is well-placed — objects appearing without explanation, consistent with the matchbook and yarn logic.

**Flags:**

**1. Meta-commentary breaks the silence it claims:**
> "The floor is warm under your feet. The bass is in your chest. These are facts, not observations."

"These are facts, not observations" is the narrator observing the quality of its own silence. It's clever but self-defeating — the voice that went quiet is commenting on how quiet it is. **Fix:** Cut "These are facts, not observations." The two preceding sentences already demonstrate the shift. Trust the reader.

**2. "A recognition" is telling:**
> "Something below the floor shifts. A warmth. A recognition."

"A recognition" is labeling an emotion/event. What does recognition feel like from underneath a floor? **Fix:** "Something below the floor shifts. A warmth. A settling, the way weight settles when something you're carrying stops being heavy."

**3. Ocean metaphor outside the Wanderer's register:**
> "Not with gratitude. With a shift in pressure, the way an ocean shifts when a river reaches it."

The ocean/river metaphor is geological-scale. The Wanderer's analogies root in lived experience — church basements, car rides, block parties. This is Familiar language (tide pools, continents). **Fix:** Ground it in something human: "With a shift in pressure, the way a room changes when someone you were waiting for walks in."

**4. The conversation is slightly too articulate:**
> "I kept the jacket on last time. Stayed at the edge. Watched everyone else. That's what I do. I watch. I hold onto things so I can describe them later. So I can prove I was here."

This is well-written but it's a speech, not dialogue. Real people in warehouses at 1am don't deliver five consecutive sentences of self-analysis this clean. One of these sentences should stumble or trail off. **Fix:** Break it up with a pause or a physical beat: "'I kept the jacket on last time. Stayed at the edge.' She's looking at the dance floor. 'I hold onto things so I can describe them later. I don't know why I do that.'"

## Summary

**Best node:** w_loading_dock. Don't touch it.

**Most flags:** w_silence_run3 and w_bartender_run3. The silence node has the hardest job — it needs to be about meta-cognition without becoming meta-narration. It mostly works but can be tightened by cutting the explanatory philosophy and trusting the structural shift (post-silence simplicity) to carry the meaning.

**Recurring issues across Run 3:**
- Systems/filter/mechanism language leaking in (bartender, silence)
- Narrating reflection instead of showing it (silence, mirror)
- Metaphors reaching beyond lived experience (ocean/river in mirror)
- Over-explaining the thematic point instead of trusting the reader

**What's consistently working:**
- Physical grounding and texture (condensation, fog, warmth, bass)
- Dialogue and character specificity (loading dock crew, jacket woman)
- Objects behaving wrong (matchbook returning, truth shard appearing)
- The structural conceit — prose getting simpler after the silence
