**Impact:** Medium. "Lens" and "filter" are Builder/systems language. The Wanderer feels changes in the body, not in signal processing. Rewrite as physical sensation.

**BEFORE:**

**AFTER:**
```

Like blinking underwater and realizing the water is clear - same room, same crowd, but the blur you didn't know was there is gone. You didn't ask for this. She didn't offer it. It just happened, the way the yarn appeared in your pocket, the way the matchbook showed everyone something different.
```

**Changes:** (1) Replaced lens/filter abstraction with a concrete physical analogy (blinking underwater). (2) Cut "The mechanism does what it needs to do" — Builder language, and the two preceding callbacks (yarn, matchbook) already establish the pattern without naming a mechanism.

### Full proposed text — w_bartender_run3:

```
The side door puts you behind the bar. Not in front of it - behind it, in the narrow corridor where the bottles are. The bartender is three feet away. She doesn't look up.

She's making something. Not a whiskey sour this time - something with no name, built from bottles you don't recognize, poured in an order that seems more like ceremony than recipe. She holds each bottle with both hands. She tilts them at precise angles. She is counting something under her breath that isn't numbers.

She slides it across the bar to you without looking. The glass is warm. The liquid inside is the color of amber held up to firelight. It smells like the ozone from the dance floor and something else - cedar, maybe, or the inside of a very old book.

You drink it. It tastes like the moment right before you remember something important. Not the memory itself - the threshold. The tip-of-the-tongue feeling, drawn out, stretched across your whole body.

Something adjusts inside you. Not your mood. Something behind your eyes, maybe. Like blinking underwater and realizing the water is clear - same room, same crowd, but the blur you didn't know was there is gone. You didn't ask for this. She didn't offer it. It just happened, the way the yarn appeared in your pocket, the way the matchbook showed everyone something different.

You step out from behind the bar. The room is the same room but you are seeing it differently. The edges are sharper. The fog is less opaque. You can see the tendons in a woman's wrist as she dances near the speakers.
```

## w_silence_run3 (THE SILENCE) — 3 edits

### Edit 4: Show recognition physically, don't tell it
**Impact:** High. Two sentences of self-analysis ("You know this because you do the same thing. You watch. You collect. You narrate the experience instead of having it.") violate the "don't narrate reflection" rule. The Wanderer should recognize the jacket woman through the body, not through a thesis.

**BEFORE:**
```
She is standing near the edge of the floor - not in it, not watching it. Her jacket is zipped. Her arms are crossed. She has built a structure around herself and she is living inside it. You know this because you do the same thing. You watch. You collect. You narrate the experience instead of having it.
```

**AFTER:**
```
She is standing near the edge of the floor - not in it, not watching it. Her jacket is zipped to the collar. Her arms are crossed. She is watching the dance floor the way you've been watching it all night - collecting, not joining. Your jaw tightens. You recognize the posture.
```

**Changes:** (1) "Built a structure around herself" → cut. The zipped jacket and crossed arms ARE the structure; naming it is redundant. Added "to the collar" for texture. (2) "You know this because you do the same thing" → "Your jaw tightens. You recognize the posture." Physical recognition instead of analytical recognition. (3) "You narrate the experience instead of having it" → "collecting, not joining." Shorter, embedded in observation, doesn't announce the theme.

### Edit 6: Shorten the gap passage
**Impact:** High. This is the climax of the node. The current version explains the gap — the fix trusts the reader to feel it.

**BEFORE:**
```
Not fades. Not trails off. Goes quiet the way a signal goes quiet when the transmitter is switched off. Between one thought and the absence of the next thought there is a gap and the gap is not empty. The gap is full. The gap is so full that the voice was the thing making it seem empty - filling the silence with description so you wouldn't notice that the silence was already complete.
```

**AFTER:**
```
Not fades. Not trails off. Goes quiet the way a signal goes quiet when the transmitter is switched off. Between one thought and the absence of the next thought there is a gap and the gap is not empty. The gap is the fullest thing you have ever felt. The voice was in the way.
```

**Changes:** Cut the explanatory philosophy ("filling the silence with description so you wouldn't notice that the silence was already complete"). That sentence tells the reader what the silence means. The rewrite lets the reader arrive there: "the voice was in the way" implies everything the longer version stated. Six words instead of twenty-eight.

### Full proposed text — w_silence_run3:

```
You are walking through the crowd and you are watching yourself walk through the crowd. The voice in your head - the one that has narrated every experience you have ever had, the one that collects and catalogues and describes - is talking.

The fog clings to the shoulders of the man in the denim jacket. The condensation on the plastic cups catches the orange light. The bass has shifted register since the Slide - darker now, more textured. A woman near the speakers is dancing with her whole body, elbows out, chin up, completely gone. The paint-knuckle man is leaning against a pillar with a drink, watching the floor the way you watch the floor. You're not sure anymore whether you notice things or the voice tells you to.

You see the woman with the jacket.

She is standing near the edge of the floor - not in it, not watching it. Her jacket is zipped to the collar. Her arms are crossed. She is watching the dance floor the way you've been watching it all night - collecting, not joining. Your jaw tightens. You recognize the posture.

She uncrosses her arms.

Not because someone asked. Not because the music demanded it. She uncrosses her arms the way you might set down a bag you forgot you were carrying. The weight was so familiar it had become invisible and now it is on the ground and her shoulders are different.

The jacket comes off. She folds it over her arm with the care of someone who understands exactly what she is removing.

And the voice - your voice, the narrator, the collector -

goes quiet.

Not fades. Not trails off. Goes quiet the way a signal goes quiet when the transmitter is switched off. Between one thought and the absence of the next thought there is a gap and the gap is not empty. The gap is the fullest thing you have ever felt. The voice was in the way.
```

### Full proposed text — w_jacket_woman:

```
You walk toward her and the voice does not describe you walking toward her. You are just walking. The floor is warm under your feet. The bass is in your chest.

She looks at you. You look at her. Neither of you says anything for a moment and the silence between you is the same silence - the one behind the voice, the one that was always complete.

"How long have you been coming here?" she asks.

You don't know how to answer that. You're not sure how many times you've been here. You're not sure it matters.

"I almost didn't come back," she says. She's looking at the dance floor, not at you. "I kept the jacket on last time. Stayed at the edge." She pushes her hair back. "I hold onto things so I can describe them later. I don't know why I do that."

She looks at you and her eyes are clear and specific.

"You do that too."

It's not a question. She's not guessing. She looked at you and saw the collector. The narrator. The one who stands slightly outside every experience and takes notes.

"I don't know what's different tonight," she says. "The jacket came off and I didn't decide to take it off. Something underneath the deciding just... did it." She looks at her hands. "It's lighter without it."

The voice has not come back. You are standing in a warehouse full of bass and fog and two hundred people and you are having a conversation with a stranger and you are not documenting it. You are not collecting this moment. You are inside it.

Something below the floor shifts. A warmth. Not from the DJ - from further down. From the place where the fractal layers go. Something down there felt what just passed between you and responded. Not with gratitude. With a shift in pressure, the way a room changes when someone you were waiting for walks in.
```
