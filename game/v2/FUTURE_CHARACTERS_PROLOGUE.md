## The Bartender
*"The glasses are mostly correct."*

The Familiar's partner. Already planted across three documents — pouring approximate drinks in f_002, revealed as the other half of a precision instrument in f_009, antlers that won't fold in f_019, the look across the empty room. She's the only character in the project who has a relationship. The Familiar has function. The Builder has craft. The Wanderer has attention. The Bartender has someone she looks at across an empty warehouse when the work is done.

**Tone:** The comedy playthrough. Lightest tone, sharpest observations. She learned drink-making from watching humans, so she's running a cargo cult within the cargo cult — imitating a ritual she doesn't understand inside a venue imitating a concept she helped design. Someone orders a manhattan and she gives them bourbon with a cherry in it because the cherry seemed important. Someone asks for "something strong" and she pours them fog fluid because it has the highest specific gravity of anything behind the bar.

**The move:** She's the only character who watches the Familiar work. The Wanderer sees the antlered woman pass by. The Familiar IS the antlered woman. The Bartender watches her partner activate, pair deposits, dissolve — and then watches her come back to the bar afterward and start wiping it down. The Bartender's closing is not dissolution. It's watching someone you've worked alongside for geological ages begin to dissolve and not being able to do anything because your hands are full of glasses and you're not sure your hands are still there either.

The look. "Familiar. Ancient. Tired in a way that has nothing to do with the body." — that line hits different from HER side.

**Species of attention:** Social observation. Human rituals of ordering, receiving, thanking. The bar as a vantage point — she sees the whole room the way the edge-path Wanderer does, but from behind a counter, through a role, with a purpose that is both disguise and function.

**Structure:** Short. 10-12 nodes. Linear. ~10 minutes.

**Unlock:** After Familiar.

**Priority:** First. She's the most playable, the funniest, and she fills the emotional gap — the only perspective that contains a relationship between two beings who chose each other. Everything else in this project is about what happens to people who didn't choose to be here. The Bartender chose. The Familiar chose. They chose each other, across geological time, and tonight they're running a nightclub together. That's the most human story in the game, told by the least human characters.

## The Jacket Woman
*"She hasn't taken it off all night despite the heat."*

Planted across three characters. The Wanderer sees armor. The Familiar sees a maintained boundary. The Dance Circle asks her to drop it. She does. What was she holding in?

**Tone:** Resistance. Not the Wanderer's uncertain-agency resistance — deliberate, conscious. She KNOWS something is wrong with this room. She felt it at the door. She came in anyway because the alternative was another night at home and she is so tired of nights at home. She holds her jacket closed through the Slide, through the Mating Ritual, through the pairing attempts. The rod responds to her and she steps away from it. She maintains the boundary.

**The move:** The circle. The arrow points at her. She enters. Not because the crowd wanted her to. Because she was tired of the weight of refusing.

This is the character for the player who identified with staying in the ring at w_010b. The player who chose wary and denies and watched_circle. The Jacket Woman is their character — someone who held back, saw the mechanism clearly, and chose to step in anyway, eyes open, knowing exactly what she was giving up.

**Species of attention:** Self-monitoring. The internal voice that watches you watching yourself. The jacket as a physical manifestation of the boundary between performing and being.

**Structure:** The most dramatic temperature arc. Cold → hot in a single moment. The jacket comes off inside the circle and the Truth yield is the highest the Familiar has measured all night.

**Artifact:** The jacket itself — she leaves it behind, draped over a chair by the door, and walks out into the cold without it. The absence of armor as the artifact. She feels everything now.

**Unlock:** After Wanderer. Possibly requires watched_circle tag to suggest thematic resonance.

## The Passed-Over
*"The rod did not respond."*

A perspective from outside the building. The negative space.

The Wanderer describes people walking parallel toward the warehouse. Who are those people? Where were they going before the frequency redirected them? There's a character in the absence — the person who was walking toward the warehouse and then walked past it. The one the frequency didn't catch. The one who felt the pressure in their sternum and kept walking.

**Tone:** Quiet devastation. Normalcy as horror. They go home. They put on a show. They fall asleep. Nothing happened. But something is wrong in the same way the escape ending is wrong — an incompletion they can't locate. They didn't refuse. They were never asked. The frequency evaluated them and moved on. In the Familiar's taxonomy, an irrelevance.

**The move:** The inverse of every other perspective. Not the experience of the harvest, but the experience of being passed over by it. The quiet devastation of not being interesting enough for the cosmic horror to bother with. The Wanderer was measured by the rod. The Jacket Woman was found by the arrow. The Passed-Over was assessed and dismissed without their knowledge.

**Species of attention:** None. That's the point. They don't notice anything because there was nothing to notice. The room they almost entered is a building on the industrial edge of town and they kept walking and the bass faded and they arrived at wherever they were going before the gradient existed and it was fine. It was fine.

**Structure:** VERY short. 5-6 nodes. No choices. No agency. The horror is in the normalcy. Probably the last thing you'd build but the thing that haunts the player who's done everything else.

**Unlock:** After all other characters. The reward for completionists. The thing that makes them set the game down and stare at the wall.

## DJ OOR
*"The arrow is not random."*

The construct itself. Pre-human logic. Already noted in the character guide — save for last. The most disorienting perspective.

**Tone:** Alien. Absurdist. The horror is in what OOR doesn't notice. Humans as mineral deposits. Music as a drill. The crowd as material to be processed. The clinical OOR action blocks that appeared in every other character's nodes — those were OOR's actual stream of consciousness. The OOR playthrough is ONLY those blocks, expanded, laid bare.

**The move:** The player reads the evening as a series of adjustments, measurements, and yield calculations. No emotion. No metaphor. Density in sector three insufficient, adjust sub-frequency by 2.4Hz, observe correction. The Slide — the moment the Wanderer experienced as transcendent surrender — described as "Unity yield spike, 11 seconds at full density, sufficient."

And then one moment where something happens that OOR can't classify. The Builder's chamfer frequency. A micro-resonance not in the spec. Something made by a human hand that the construct did not predict. The OOR playthrough's emotional turn is a measurement anomaly — a data point that doesn't fit. OOR's response — opening the glove, showing the heat sink — is not gratitude. It's the construct encountering something it needs to catalogue and finding no category. The closest thing OOR has to wonder, expressed as a display of an unclassifiable component.

**Breaking the convention:** OOR doesn't have a second-person perspective in the way the others do. "You" implies a self. OOR is a construct. The prose would need to break the second-person convention or use it in a way that makes the reader uncomfortable — "you" addressed to something that doesn't experience subjectivity. "You adjust the low-pass filter. The word 'you' does not apply to what you are. You use it because the format requires it."

**Species of attention:** Measurement. Pure operational awareness. No metaphor, no texture, no interiority. Just data. The species of attention is the absence of attention — a system processing inputs. Until the chamfer. Until the anomaly.

**Structure:** Structurally different from all other playthroughs. Possibly non-linear. Possibly formatted as a log. The game looking at itself.

**Unlock:** After all other characters. Requires a specific artifact combination. The player who gets here has seen every side of the evening. Now they see the machine's side. And the machine is not what they expected.
